EL NIDO

When it comes to pristine white sand beaches and islands, El Nido in Palawan comes highly-recommended by local and international travelers as the top choice to go to when in the Philippines.

El Nido has been named a number of times by travel publication sites as the best island beaches in the world because of its paradise-like attractions with white sand, turquoise waters, vibrant coral reefs, and stunning limestone cliffs.

El Nido the gateway to the awe-inspiring Bacuit Archipelago which houses 45 islands and islets, each with its unique geological formations. It’s no wonder that El Nido has been ranked consistently as one of the best island destinations not just in the Philippines, but in the world. There are a lot of things to do in El Nido from island-hopping, lounging on beaches, hiking up cliffs, and even scuba diving. Reading this guide on El Nido, Palawan will make it easy for you to plan a trip and maximize your time in one of the best tourist spots in the Philippines.

Secret Lagoon



Secret Lagoon

From the boat, you cannot see the Secret Lagoon, which is about the only part of this lagoon that is much of a secret. It’s a pretty popular spot and there is often a line to get inside. Your boat will dock and you will walk along the reef (kind of unfortunate parking spot and setup) towards the beach and then head over to the Secret Lagoon entrance. It’s just a small hole in the limestone wall and when you squeeze through it opens up into a small pool of water completely surrounded by the cliffs.

Nacpan Beach



Nacpan Beach

Nacpan Beach is a four-kilometer strip of cream-colored sand and coconut trees with crystal clear turquoise water. It is located 17th kilometers north of the El Nido town proper in Palawan. This powdery paradise was one of the secret getaways for some tourists until its beauty was discovered by many andeventually became one of the most sought after destinations in El Nido. In 2016, it was voted as the world’s 10th best beach by users of travel rating website Trip Advisor.
